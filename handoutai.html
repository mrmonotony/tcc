3

Evaluating Web Sources

1. Check the Domain

Look at the URL ending:

.gov / .edu - High credibility
.org - Check organizational bias
.com / .net - Commercial / Ads

2. Lateral Reading

Don't stay on the site. Open a new tab and search what other credible sources say about the author or organization.

3. The Wikipedia Hack

Never cite Wikipedia directly. Scroll to the bottom References section to find original academic studies!

Source Credibility Assessment Cheat Sheet

Green Light (High Credibility)
  • Peer-reviewed journal articles
  • Official government agency reports
  • Academic books from university presses
Red Flags (Exercise Caution)
  • No named author or credentials
  • Heavy presence of clickbait ads
  • Unverified social media posts

MLA Citation Quick Check (9th Edition)

Standard formula for a journal article from a TCC Library database:

Author Last, First. "Article Title." Journal Title, vol. #, no. #, Year, pp. #-#. Database Name, https://doi.org/xx.xxxx.
